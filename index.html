PI-00 · Introduction

An engineer who treats good processes as a craft.

I'm Akash — a chemical and process engineer who likes problems that sit between the theoretical and the physical. A column that won't converge, a line that bottlenecks at one station, a duct that drops more pressure than it should. The interesting work is in closing the gap between what the model says and what the plant actually does.

Originally from Chennai, India and now based in Padova, Italy, I spent two years at Carborundum Universal (CUMI) turning that instinct into measured results — lifting hot-press wheel production by 150%, mapping value streams, and building the dashboards that let three sites finally see their own numbers. Now I'm at the University of Padova for an MSc, working across process design, separation systems, and machine learning for process engineers.

I care about sustainability and rigour in equal measure — knowing precisely which number a method supports, and which is an extrapolation. That habit of checking my own assumptions is the part of engineering I enjoy most.
